What is Pull Over Machine

The Pull Over Machine is a gym apparatus designed to target your back muscles, specifically your lats and to a lesser extent your chest. Unlike free weight pullovers with dumbbells or barbells, the machine offers a more controlled movement path, making it ideal for beginners or those recovering from injuries. By adjusting the seat and handles, you can customize the exercise to target different areas of your back. During the exercise, you'll pull the handle down in an arc towards your chest, mimicking the pulling motion of traditional pullovers but with added stability and support.

How to Use

To use the Pull Over Machine, adjust the seat and handle for comfort, then lie down with your back pressed against the pad, grasp the handles with an overhand grip, and lower them in a controlled arc towards your chest before raising them back up to the starting position while maintaining a tight core and a straight back.

DO's

  • Ensure the seat is positioned so your back is flat against the pad and your arms are slightly bent when holding the handles. Choose a handle position that feels comfortable and targets the desired back muscles (lats for wider grip, chest for narrower grip).
  • Keep your core engaged throughout the movement, back pressed against the pad, and spine straight. Breathe out as you lower the weight and inhale as you bring it back up.
  • Lower and raise the handles in a controlled arc, focusing on using your back muscles to perform the movement, not momentum.
  • Lower the handles down towards your chest until you feel a stretch in your lats, but avoid hyperextending your back at the top.
  • Select a weight that allows you to perform 10-15 controlled repetitions with good form.

DON'Ts

  • It's better to start lighter and focus on proper form than risk injury by using too much weight.
  • Keep your core engaged and back straight to avoid back strain.
  • Focus on using your back muscles to move the weight, not momentum.
  • Maintain a slight bend in your elbows throughout the movement to avoid hyperextension.
  • Breathe steadily throughout the exercise.
  • Keep your shoulders relaxed and down throughout the movement.
